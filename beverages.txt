aam ka panna

Ingredients:

1. 3 medium size green raw mangoes
2. 1 cup Sugar 
3. 1 tblsp roasted cumin powder
4. Black salt to taste 
5. Salt to taste
6. 2 cup water
7. 1 tblsp Mint leaves (pudina patta)
8. Ice crushed

How to make aam panna:

1. Place the mangoes along with water in a sauce-pan and bring to a boil. Simmer for 10 minutes.<br>
2. Then remove from fire Alternately you can pressure cook the mangoes with water till soft.<br>
3. Let them cool and then peel the mangoes and get the pulp with the help of a spoon.<br> 
4. Grind mango pulp along with water to a smooth paste.<br>
5. In a pan take the pulpy mixture, add sugar and let it cook on flame till the sugar dissolves completely.<br> 
6. Stir continously lest you burn the mixture.<br>
7. Remove from fire and add roasted cumin powder, black salt and salt.<br>
8. Let this mixture cool down properly.<br>
9. To make the drink : take a tall glass pour 1 -2 tblsp of the mango mixture and pour in chilled water till 3/4 th.<br> 
10. Now add finely chopped mint  and crushed ice.<br>
11. Garnish with mint leaves and serve immediately.<br>

chass

ingredients 
1. 5 cups curd/yogurt <br>
2. half tsp roasted cumin powder/bhuna jeera powder or add as required<br>
3. 1 cup water<br>
4. 5 to 6 small ice cubes<br>
6. black salt/kala namak as required<br>
7. a few coriander leaves or mint leaves for garnish<br>

. how to make the recipe:
1. take all the ingredients in a blender jar, except the coriander leaves.<br>
2. just pulse the blender for some seconds till everything is blended well and the mixture becomes frothy.<br>
3. pour chaas in glasses.<br>
4. garnish with coriander leaves and serve chaas immediately.<br>
5. you can also chill the butter milk in the refrigerator and serve later. but use it in the same day<br>


falooda


1. half cup falooda sev<br>
2. 4 to 5 tbsp soaked sabja seeds/sweet basil seeds (soak about ½ tbsp sabja seeds in enough water)<br>
3. 4 cups chilled whole milk<br>
4. 4 scoops of vanilla ice cream<br>
5. 5 to 6 tbsp rose syrup<br>
6. 5 to 6 shelled pistachios, sliced or slivered<br>
7. 5 to 6 cashews or almonds, sliced<br>
8. 5 to 6 golden raisins<br>

how to make the recipe:

1. soak ½ tbsp of sabja seeds in water for about 30 to 40 mins. once they swell. drain and then keep aside.<br>
2. prepare the falooda sev according to package instructions.<br>
3. if cooking the sev, then once cooked, rinse the cooked falooda sev in water. drain and keep them aside. let them cool completely.<br>
4. in 4 glasses each, add 1.5 to 2 tbsp rose syrup. depending upon your taste you can add less or more. 2 tbsp of rose syrup makes it more sweet.
then add 1 or 1.5 tbsp of sabja seeds<br>
5. add 1 to 1.5 tbsp of the falooda sev.<br>
6. in a slow stream, pour about ¾ to 1 cup milk.<br>
7. then top with one scoop of vanilla ice cream.<br>
8. garnish with a few raisins, sliced pistachios, almonds or cashews.<br>
9.serve the falooda immediately.<br>


Lassi

1. 2 cups chilled fresh curd/dahi or yogurt <br>
2. 5 to 2 cups chilled water or milk <br>
3. 10 to 12 tbsp sugar or add as per taste <br>
4. 1 tsp cardamom powder or 2 tsp rose water <br>
5. a few strands of saffron/kesar (optional)<br>
6. 6 to 8 ice cubes (optional)<br>
7. 1 to 2 tbsp chopped or sliced dry fruits (optional)<br>

how to make the recipe:

1. in a mortar, take 8 to 10 cardamom pods and crush them. remove the husk and crush the seeds. <br>
2. crush to a fine powder.<br>
3. in a bowl or pan, take 2 cups fresh chilled yogurt. i used homemade curd.<br>
4. with a madani or blender or wired whisk just mix the curd till it become smooth.<br>
5. the curd should be smooth before you add other ingredients to it.<br>
6. add 10 to 12 tbsp sugar or as required.<br>
7. then add 2 cups chilled water. you can also add 2 cups chilled milk, instead of water. the consistency of this lassi is neither thin nor thick. <br>
8. if you want a thick lassi, then add 1 to 1.5 cups water. <br>
9. now churn the madani in the lassi mixture. <br>
10. keep on churning till the sugar dissolves and you see a nice frothy layer on top. you can also use an immersion blender for the same. <br>
11. another way is to add everything in a blender jar and blend till smooth. <br>
12. now add 1 tsp of the crushed cardamom powder and a few saffron strands. as a substitute for cardamom powder, you can also add 2 tsp of rose water. <br>
13. you can also skip all the flavoring ingredients, if you don't have any.<br>
14. just mix with the madani again. add ice cubes and mix again.<br>
15. pour lassi in tall glasses and serve. if you want, you can also top the lassi with 1 to 2 tbsp of malai (cream which collect on top of milk) or 1 to 2 tbsp of unsalted white butter/makhan.<br>
16. garnish lassi with a pinch or two of cardamom powder, 2 to 3 saffron strands or chopped dry fruits. serve sweet punjabi lassi immediately.<br>

Masala chai
1. 1 tsp. or 5 grams Green cardamoms skinned (elaichi)<br>
2. half tsp or 2 grams cloves / lavang<br>
3. 5 to 3 grams cinnamon (dalchini)<br>
4. one fourth tsp. or 1.5 grams fennel seeds (saunf)<br>
5. 1/4 to 1/2 tsp pepper corn or 1/2 tsp. powder<br>
6. 1/2 tsp. nutmeg grated or 1/3 of large nutmeg<br>
7. 2 petals of star flower / star anise (chakri phool)<br>
8. 3/4cup milk (180 ml)<br>
9. 1 cup water (240 ml)<br>
10. half tsp. masalapowder (you can increase slightly for stronger tea)<br>
11. tsp. tea powder (adjust to suit your taste)<br>
12. 1 tsp. freshly chopped or crushed ginger<br>
13. Sweetener as needed<br>


Making the tea powder

1. Add all the spices to blender jar and powder finely.<br>
2. Store it in an airtight glass jar.<br>
3. How to make masala tea using the spice powder.<br>
4. Bring one cup of water to a boil.<br>
5. Add crushed ginger, spice powder and tea powder. Boil for about 2 to 3 minutes or till the flavors are infused well.<br>
6. Add milk and simmer for 2 to 3 minutes.<br>
7. Filter it to the serving cups.<br>
8. Serve hot or warm.<br>

Jal Jeera

1. 1 tbsp seedless tamarind - for a pronounced more sourness, you can add ½ tbsp of more tamarind.<br>
2. 3/4 cup mint leaves/pudina patta<br>
3. 1 or 1.5 tsp cumin seeds/jeera<br>
4. 1 tsp fennel seeds/saunf<br>
5. 1/2 tsp black pepper<br>
6. 1/2 or 1 tsp amchur/dry mango powder<br>
7. seeds removed from 1 black cardamom (optional)<br>
8. a pinch of asafoetida/hing (optional)<br>
9. 1 tbsp boondi<br>
10. 1/2 or 1 tsp chaat masala powder (optional)<br>
11. black salt or rock salt as required<br>

how to make the recipe:
1. rinse the mint leaves.<br>
2. also rinse the tamarind ball.<br>
3. first blend all the ingredients except boondi and chaat masala in 1/2 or 3/4 cup water till smooth.<br>
then strain through a strainer<br>
4. add 3-4 cups of chilled water to the strained mixture or as required<br>
5. keep in the fridge or serve chilled jaljeera.<br>
6. add boondi and a pinch of chaat masala to jaljeera while serving.<br>

nimbupani
1. 1 medium size lemon<br>
2. 2 glasses of water<br>
3. 1 tsp cumin powder<br>
4. rock salt or black salt as required<br>
6. sugar as required<br>
7. 4-5 mint leaves for garnishing - optional<br>
8. a few ice cubes<br>
how to make the recipe:

1. cut the lemon in to two.<br>
2. take 2 glasses of a water in a bowl<br>
3. with a lemon juice squeezer, squeeze the juice directly into the water.<br>
4. add black salt, sugar, jeera powder.<br>
5. stir till the sugar dissolves.<br>
6. pour the shikanji in glasses. add ice cubes. garnish with mint and serve shikanji immediately.<br>
7. you can also make the shikanji and chill in the fridge and then serve.<br>

Haldi doodh

1. an inch piece of turmeric root, grated or 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder<br>
2. 220 ml of milk<br>
3. honey/ sugar to taste<br>
4. optionally freshly pounded black pepper<br>

1. To begin making the Turmeric Milk (Haldi Doodh), bring the grated turmeric and milk to boil for a couple of minutes. <br>
2. Once it boils, turn off the heat and allow the turmeric milk mixture to rest covered for a couple of more minutes.<br>  
3. This will bring out the essential medicines and color from the turmeric. <br>
4. After a couple of minutes of resting, strain the milk from the turmeric into a glass, stir in sugar / honey and black pepper and serve the Turmeric Milk while still hot.<br>
